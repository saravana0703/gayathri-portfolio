Who I am

I am a Senior Presentation Designer specializing in financial storytelling and board-ready communication. My work sits at the intersection of design, strategy, and business, translating complex models, risk narratives, and strategic roadmaps into presentations that leaders can act on.

Career journey

Starting as a Document Specialist supporting Boston Consulting Group at RR Donnelley, I learned the rigor of consulting-grade communication and XML-driven automation. At Williams Lea – Bank of America, I refined high-volume presentation production, template systems, and process optimization. Today, as a Senior Presentation Designer for Goldman Sachs, I own end-to-end executive presentations—from investor decks to board reporting—earning a promotion within three months.

Professional philosophy

Every slide is a decision surface. My role is to ensure that leaders see the signal, not the noise. I design for clarity, confidence, and control, so executives can walk into any room—board, investor, regulator—with a narrative that stands up to questions.

Design thinking process

  1. Decode the business problem – Understand the decision, audience, and risk.
  2. Structure the narrative – Architect the storyline, hierarchy, and flow.
  3. Model the data visually – Use ThinkCell, Excel, and Power BI to reveal insight.
  4. Design for executive consumption – Minimal, precise, and brand-aligned.
  5. Stress-test the story – Anticipate questions, objections, and alternative views.

Case Study 01 · Investment Banking Pitch Book

Challenge

A coverage team needed a deal pitch book for a time-sensitive client meeting, consolidating market analysis, valuation scenarios, and transaction structures into a single, coherent narrative.

Approach

Re-architected the deck into a decision-led storyline, prioritized key valuation views, and used ThinkCell to build scenario and sensitivity charts directly from Excel models.

Solution

Delivered a visually minimal, analytically rich pitch book with clear sections for context, options, and recommendations, aligned with brand and compliance standards.

Outcome

The team reported higher client engagement, faster walkthroughs, and reduced time spent explaining complex charts during the meeting.

Case Study 02 · Board-Level Strategy Presentation

Challenge

A business unit needed to present a multi-year strategy to the board, balancing ambition with risk, investment, and regulatory constraints.

Approach

Partnered with strategy and finance teams to define a top-down narrative, mapping initiatives to financial impact and risk appetite, while simplifying dense backup content.

Solution

Created a board-ready deck with a crisp executive summary, strategic pillars, roadmap visuals, and clearly framed trade-offs.

Outcome

The board session ran to time, with fewer clarification questions and clear alignment on next steps and investment priorities.

Case Study 03 · Investor Communication Deck

Challenge

Leadership needed a quarterly investor update that reconciled financial performance, risk metrics, and strategic initiatives into a single, coherent message.

Approach

Consolidated inputs from finance, risk, and strategy teams, then built a storyline around momentum, resilience, and discipline, supported by consistent visual frameworks.

Solution

Designed a modular deck that could be reused across investor calls, leadership meetings, and regulatory conversations.

Outcome

Reduced preparation time for subsequent quarters and improved message consistency across external communications.

Case Study 04 · Executive Data Visualization Dashboard

Challenge

Senior leadership needed a single-view dashboard to monitor key performance and risk indicators across multiple business lines.

Approach

Collaborated with analytics teams to define critical metrics, then prototyped dashboards in Power BI and translated them into presentation-ready views.

Solution

Delivered a dashboard-style presentation with consistent chart language, color coding, and drill-down paths for leadership discussions.

Outcome

Enabled faster executive reviews and more focused discussions on outliers and emerging risks.
